# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters, potential clients, and collaborators who arrive via link or search. They have 15-30 seconds before deciding whether to keep reading. They're evaluating craft, taste, and technical range — not reading a resume.

## Product Purpose

A portfolio for Ariel González, full stack developer. Its purpose is to demonstrate creative depth and technical ability in one motion. The first frame must earn the next scroll. Success means a visitor stays, forwards the link, and reaches out.

## Brand Personality

Creativo, experimental, audaz. Not trying to be safe — trying to be remembered. The portfolio itself is a proof of skill: how it moves, what it chooses not to show, and how copy lands is part of the work.

## References

- **Franky Hung** — character-driven motion, typographic play that morphs into abstraction, the transition itself is the concept
- **Studio Karo** — editorial restraint + surprise, white space used aggressively, then broken
- **Tigranz** — raw energy, system-aware motion, nothing decorative that isn't also structural
- **Linear** — dark-native, obsessive craft in micro-interactions, the product looks inevitable

What to capture specifically: the Franky Hung morphic title transition — type becomes shape becomes type again. Linear's restraint. Tigranz's confidence.

## Anti-references

- Generic React Bootstrap portfolio: hero with circular photo, identical project cards, experience timeline, corporate blue
- Landing page of SaaS in disguise: big metrics, social proof, "Start for free" CTAs — looks like a product, not a person
- Over-designed Awwwards-clone 2023: cursor halo, every heading split-animated, black + fuchsia, horizontal scroll as default

## Design Principles

1. **The transition is the message.** The typographic-to-motionGraphic morph is not decoration — it expresses what a full stack dev does: builds things that work at multiple levels of abstraction.
2. **Restraint earns the moment.** Everything quiet makes the animated moment hit harder. Don't animate what the eye doesn't need.
3. **Dark without decorating the darkness.** Near-black base with no gradients, no glows, no glassmorphism as default. Let contrast do the work.
4. **Show, don't describe.** No "passionate about code" copy. The motion is the argument.
5. **Structural motion only.** Every animated element must carry information or reveal sequence. If removing it loses nothing, cut it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA minimum (4.5:1 for body, 3:1 for large text)
- Full `prefers-reduced-motion` support: crossfade fallback for the morph animation, no flash
- Keyboard navigable navbar
- All decorative SVG shapes marked `aria-hidden`
